EAGE 2nd Borehole Geophysics Workshop and GeoConvention 2013: A High Resolution VSP In The Oil Sands For Improved Reservoir Characterization – Mark Morrish and Laurie Bellman

The University of Calgary CREWES consortium was involved with a project HDSC acquired in the winter of 2011/12.  A 26 page research paper was published that studied the dynamite and vibroseis VSP data, concluding “This is a high-quality, very rich dataset, that will afford many opportunities for future research”.

CREWES has published over 20 papers and presentations using this dataset, with more coming. The convention abstracts are more condensed, whereas the CREWES reports are longer and more detailed:

HDVSP Features at a Glance

  • 3-component
  • MEMS sensors
  • 1 or 2 metre sensor interval
  • 100's of sensors per well
  • Up to 720m (current maximum)
  • 1ms sample rate
  • 2D, 3D & 4D
  • Multi-well VSPs

Standalone or simultaneous recording with surface seismic
